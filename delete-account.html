Delete Your Account

App: Once a Day
Developer: One Question

You have the right to delete your account and associated data at any time. This page explains how to delete your account and what happens to your data.

How to Delete Your Account

  1. Open the Once a Day app
    Launch the app on your device and make sure you're logged in.
  2. Go to Settings
    Tap on the Settings icon (gear icon) in the bottom navigation or profile menu.
  3. Select "Delete Account"
    Scroll down to find the "Delete Account" option and tap on it.
  4. Confirm deletion
    Read the information about what will be deleted, then confirm your decision. You may need to re-authenticate for security purposes.
⚠️ Important: Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Please make sure you want to proceed before confirming.

What Happens to Your Data

When you delete your account, your data is processed as follows:

Data Type Action Retention Period
Email address Permanently deleted Immediate
Nickname / Display name Permanently deleted Immediate
Profile picture / Avatar Permanently deleted Immediate
Authentication tokens Permanently deleted Immediate
Push notification tokens Permanently deleted Immediate
Answers to daily questions Anonymized (author shown as "Deleted User") Content retained, authorship removed
Comments Anonymized (author shown as "Deleted User") Content retained, authorship removed
Reactions (likes, etc.) Permanently deleted Immediate
Activity history Permanently deleted Immediate
Backup copies Retained temporarily Up to 30 days (disaster recovery)

Why Some Content is Anonymized

Your answers and comments contribute to community discussions. To preserve the integrity of these conversations, we anonymize this content rather than delete it. The content remains visible but is no longer associated with your account—it will be displayed as coming from "Deleted User".

Backup Retention

For disaster recovery purposes, our database backups may contain your data for up to 30 days after deletion. After this period, your data will be completely purged from all systems.
